10 Technical SEO Fixes
That Can Double Your Traffic

✍ Afroza Ahmed 📅 March 2026 ⏱ 5 min read
Most websites lose rankings due to simple technical issues that are easy to fix but often overlooked. From broken internal links and slow page speed to missing canonical tags and crawl errors — these hidden problems silently kill your organic traffic. Here are the exact 10 technical SEO fixes I apply to every client audit.
01 Critical

Fix Your Page Speed

Google uses page speed as a direct ranking factor. A slow website loses both rankings and visitors. Use Google PageSpeed Insights to check your score — aim for 90+ on mobile.

Quick wins: Compress images using tools like TinyPNG, enable browser caching, and minify CSS/JS files. These three changes alone can boost your score significantly.

02 High Impact

Fix Broken Internal Links

Broken links waste your crawl budget and create a poor user experience. Use Screaming Frog to crawl your site and identify all 404 errors. Then either redirect them (301) or update the links.

Even one broken link on a high-authority page can hurt your rankings.

03 Critical

Add Missing ALT Tags to Images

Image ALT tags help Google understand what your images are about. Missing ALT tags mean missed opportunities for image search traffic. Go through every image on your site and add descriptive, keyword-rich ALT text.

Example: Instead of img001.jpg, use "SEO audit checklist for e-commerce websites".

04 High Impact

Set Up a Custom 404 Page

A generic 404 error page causes visitors to leave immediately. A custom 404 page keeps them on your site by offering helpful navigation options and links to your best content.

This reduces bounce rate and keeps your visitors engaged even when they land on a broken URL.

05 Critical

Optimize Your Meta Titles & Descriptions

Your meta title should be 50–60 characters and include your primary keyword near the beginning. Your meta description should be under 160 characters, compelling, and include a call to action.

These directly affect your click-through rate (CTR) in Google search results — even without changing your ranking position, better CTR means more traffic.

06 Medium Impact

Fix Your Heading Structure (H1–H3)

Every page should have exactly one H1 tag containing your main keyword. Use H2s for main sections and H3s for subsections. A clear heading structure helps both Google and users understand your content.

Avoid using headings just for styling — they carry SEO weight.

07 Critical

Submit & Maintain Your XML Sitemap

Your XML sitemap tells Google which pages exist on your site. Submit it in Google Search Console and check regularly for errors. Remove any noindexed pages, paginated URLs, or redirected URLs from your sitemap.

08 High Impact

Fix Duplicate Content Issues

Duplicate content confuses Google — it doesn't know which page to rank. Use canonical tags (rel="canonical") to tell Google which version of a page is the "master" version.

This is especially important for e-commerce sites with product filters and sorting URLs.

09 Medium Impact

Ensure Your Site is Mobile-Friendly

Google uses mobile-first indexing — meaning it primarily crawls and indexes the mobile version of your site. Use Google's Mobile-Friendly Test tool to check your site and fix any issues with viewport, tap targets, or font sizes.

10 High Impact

Add Schema Markup

Schema markup (structured data) helps Google understand your content and can earn you rich snippets in search results — like star ratings, FAQs, and breadcrumbs. These visually stand out and dramatically improve CTR.

Start with Person, Organization, Article, or Product schema depending on your site type.
